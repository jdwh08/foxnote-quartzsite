### Definition

For some system (e.g., a 3x3 grid of tiles) where we want to prove something is true after any sequence of steps, we look for some property that remains true (called an **[[Invariant]]**) and show that it is true at the beginning and after each possible type of step.

Example usage: prove that a sliding tile puzzle can never be rearranged to be in order, etc.

---
### NOTE:

For #math/discrete_math, these usually take the form of [[Proof by Induction]].
