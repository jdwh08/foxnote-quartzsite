# Definition:
Company valuation comes in different forms.

- [[Intrinsic Company Value]]: focuses on dividends (and maybe buybacks)
	- [[Discounted Cash Flow]]
- [[Book Value]]: focuses on the assets the company has.
- [[Market Cap]]: focuses on the current market price.
- [[Arbitrage Pricing Theory]]: focuses on ensuring two things which are the same can have the same price.

---
# Notes:
A number of fundamental trading strategies exist where we correct differences between the company's "true" value and its current stock price.

Information is supposed to affect company valuation, e.g., different expected future cash flows.
- Company Level: CEO issue
- Sector Level: sector-wide demand decrease
- Market Level: recession

---
# Examples:
- Market Cap vs Book Value: Having a Price-to-Book (MktCap/Book) below 1 suggests you could buy the entire company and sell off the pieces to make money. This should be rare.
