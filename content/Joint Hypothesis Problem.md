# Definition:

Any test of the hypothesis is checking both the hypothesis and the effectiveness of the test.

---
# Notes:

Originally this was a defense of the [[Efficient Markets Hypothesis]], by Fama.
> Tests must be based on a model of equilibrium, and any test is a joint test of efficiency and of the model of equilibrium. [Fama, 1976]([https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1976.tb03205.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1976.tb03205.x))


I LOVE this quote since it reminds us how there are two parts to every test.
1. The hypothesis
2. The test effectiveness

It gives [[Bayes' Rule]] vibes, e.g., a medical test that is 99.99% effective for a 1/50MM disease giving False.
