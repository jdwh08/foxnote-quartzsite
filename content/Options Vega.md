### Definition:
Change in price of option due to a 1% change in implied volatility of the underlying.

---
### Notes:
- Future dated options are positive Vega. Near-expiry options have negative Vega, since options holders pay larger premium for longer duration.
- Vega will change when underlying volatility will change.
- To calculate the implied volatility, you need an options pricing model like [[Black-Scholes]].
- You can compare Vega against the bid-ask spread of the option to see if the spread is competitive or not.
