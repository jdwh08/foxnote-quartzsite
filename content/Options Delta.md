### Definition:
Change in price of option given change in underlying.

---
### Notes:
- Tells the "hedging ratio" for [[Delta Hedging]] to be delta neutral. Long the underlying is equivalent to Delta of 1.
- Is also the probability the option will finish in the money.
- Delta of $\pm 0.5$ means exactly at the money. (Plus is for calls.)
- As option approaches expiration, the Delta approaches 0. Related to [[Options Theta]].
- Must be calculated using some pricing model, e.g., Black-Scholes (though actual finance people use more sophisticated models).
- Can be used to trade Delta Spreads, e.g., with Calendar Spreads, etc.
