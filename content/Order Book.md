# Definition:
The currently open list of [[Order (Finance)|orders]] at the exchange.

We have the market price, some ASK prices below it and some BID prices above. Market prices change as new orders match the BID / ASK.

Brokers will route your order to the best price for each order. 
- Typically, brokers will prefer to route through internal buy/sell orders (cheaper), but this must be at least as good as the external brokers. This also must be registered, typically with the broker where the stock was listed.
- Dark Pools are intermediaries between brokers. They pay for orders from brokers. If an order is profitable, the Dark Pool will take the order up before it hits to the exchanges.
- 90\%+ of retail exchanges go within broker or through a dark pool. Note that these prices are at least as good as market.
