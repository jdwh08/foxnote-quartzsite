### Definition:
Moments help to characterize the distribution of a random variable.

For some [[Random Variable]] X, 
- $E(X^k)$ is the **kth moment of X**, for k > 1. The first moment of X is thus the same as the [[Expected Value]].
- $E[(X-\mu)^k]$ is the **kth moment about the mean** of X. The second moment about the mean is the same as the [[Variance]].

Moments can be obtained using [[Moment Generating Functions]].

---
### Notes:
The first moment about the mean, $E(X- \mu)$ is always zero.

If the [[Probability Mass Function]] or [[Probability Density Function]] are symmetric around the mean, then typically the 3rd moment, or [[Skew]] $E[(X-\mu)^3]=0$.
