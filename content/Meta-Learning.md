## Marty Lobdell’s “Study Less Study Smart”:
1. When you are no longer able understand or follow the materials, take a short break. Most college freshmen aren’t able to go more than 30 minutes.
2. Create your own environment and space specifically for studying.
3. When learning, aim to learn the underlying concept rather than just the rote facts.
	1. But if you really need to learn the rote facts, e.g., anatomy parts, use mnemonics (acronyms, sayings, interactions…).
		1. Acronyms: ROYGBIV for colours.
		2. Sayings: In 1492, Columbus sailed the ocean blue.
		3. Interactions: Carbohydrates have four calories per gram, and cars have four wheels. Fat has 9 calories per gram, and cats have nine lives (think of fat cats). The weirder the image, the easier to remember.
4. Be active in learning. Figure out how something fits within your current web of knowledge.
5. Try to study with others.
6. Don’t highlight text blindly. Be sure you’re doing more than just recognizing the stuff you’ve highlighted as important, since we are very good at recognizing things as something we have seen before without actually understanding the material.
7. Always take notes. After you are finished, shortly afterward go back through your notes and review them / flesh them out.
8. Try teaching others the materials as a way to ensure you understand it.
9. When using a textbook, use the SQ4R method: survey, question, read, recite, review.
	1. Survey: go through the textbook and look briefly.
	2. Question: As you survey, raise questions.
	3. Read.
	4. Recite. (The “teach others” thing from above.)
	5. Review.
10. Sleep!
