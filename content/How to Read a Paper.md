# Notes:

1. Pick your reading goal. 
	1. Reading Papers? Look at figures and ask the six questions.
	2. Entering new field? Read beginning and conclusion.
	3. Reading for an Author? Skim and consider broader history.
	4. Reading for a Keyword? Focus on methods and approach.
	5. Reading for a Journal Club? Look at figures. Consider interpretations and alternatives. 
2. Understand the author's goal.
	1. Interests, Field, Techniques, Context within Field or Author's work.
	2. What kind of article is this? Methods? Review? Commentary? Resource? Research?
3. Six Basic Questions
	1. What do authors want to know?
	2. What did authors do?
	3. Why was it done that way? (relative to field)
	4. What are the results showing?
	5. How do authors interpret results?  
	6. What should be done next?
4. Look at each figure and table.
	1. Look at data and see how it was obtained.
	2. Ask why figure was structured that way.
	3. Ask what table groups are.
5. Keep in mind formatting for what each section is for.
6. Think critically about alternatives or biases.
7. Give benefit of the doubt for small mistakes.
8. Dig into supplemental materials or cited references; do multiple readings
9. Talk about results in journal club or teach others.
10. Incorporate work into other papers and your future work.
