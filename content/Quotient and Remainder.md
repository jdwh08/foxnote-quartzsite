### Definition:
Informally: given two numbers, 

If $n$ and $d$ are integers and $d > 0$, then there exists a unique pair of integers $q, r$ such that $n = qd + r$ and $0 \leq r < d$.
- $q$ is the quotient.
- $r$ is the remainder.

---
### Notes:
General Principle of Remainder Arithmetic:
To find the remainder on division $n$ after a series of additions and multiplications,
1. Replace each integer by remainder after division on $n$.
2. To ensure the results are in the range (0, n] by replacing any results by their remainder after division on $n$.
