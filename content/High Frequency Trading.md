# Definition:
A style of trading for how hedge funds can exploit [[Order Book]]s.

Version 1: Frontrunning:
1. Retail places lots of orders for a stock.
2. HFT Hedge Fund can see the high buying pressure and predicts stock will go up. It buys first. (it is closer so a couple milliseconds faster.)
3. You buy.
4. Price keeps going up.
5. HFT Hedge Fund sells you the stock and makes a small profit.

Version 2: Geographic Brokerage Arbitrage:
1. HFT sees that prices are very slightly different in one exchange (e.g., NYSE) are different from another (e.g,. LSE).
2. HFT will buy cheap and sell expensive.
