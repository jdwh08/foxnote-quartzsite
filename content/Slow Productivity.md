### Definition:
Cal Newport argues that a less frenetic approach to work results in more and higher quality productive output for knowledge workers.

---
### Notes:
- White Collar "Productivity" tends to be associated with looking busy, because it's difficult to measure directly.
- Frenetic busy-ness results in burnout and unsustainable work.
- Deep, high quality knowledge work is often produced when people take their time, slow down, and purposefully focus on a task.
- Being assigned multiple missions, and multiple projects within a mission, results in "administrative overhead" (e.g., setting up meetings), which drains time away from actual work. Narrow down this as much as possible (i.e., take only 1-2 missions, 1-3 projects, and focus on one meaningful project per day.). "Free yourself from the small." Block time to ensure you have enough, and use these estimates to reject less meaningful work. For overhead you have to do, create an "autopilot schedule": at this time, at this location, during this ritual, do X task; and get used to it until your brain does it on autopilot. Shift overhead discussion into dedicated regular "office hour" meetings, or at the team level into "docket-clearning" meetings. Disincentivize people pushing trivial work at you by making the cost higher (e.g., ask people write the full request with details into a shared list or Trello, have people do a little of the work themselves to distribute the load away from you). Avoid tasks which generate a bunch of small urgent requests (e.g. planning a conference). Be willing to pay for software or ops managers to offload these tasks for you. 
- For managers, move towards a "pull" based system where teams request work, to prevent slower sections from having work pile up, and to monitor which sections are moving too quickly.
