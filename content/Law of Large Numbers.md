### Definition:
If a random experiment is repeated independently more and more times, the average of the observed results will converge to the true [[Expected Value|Expected Value]].

Formally: Given repeated samples of a [[Independent and Identically Distributed]] [[Random Variable]], the sample [[Expected Value]] should converge to the true population mean.

$$\bar{X_n} = \frac{X_1+...+X_n}{n} \rightarrow \mu \text{ as } n\rightarrow \infty$$

### Examples:
Flip a coin and count the proportion of heads. As $n\rightarrow\infty$ , this proportion should converge to the actual proportion (0.5 for a fair coin).
