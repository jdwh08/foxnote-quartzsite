# Definition:
Getting an estimate by repeatedly taking samples of the data (usually with replacement).

Allows for [[Monte Carlo Simulation]] to estimate almost any statistic.

---
# Notes:
Proposed by Bradley Efron in "Bootstrap Methods: Another Look at the Jackknife" (1979).

We infer about a population from a sample by treating its true probability distribution as analogous to the empirical distribution.
